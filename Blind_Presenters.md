# Blind Presenters

This directory lists 14 people who are blind or have low vision and who lead in time-based media: podcasts, talks and keynotes, video, broadcast, and film. It is meant in part as a resource for aspiring blind presenters, and as a way to find and reach the people listed.

This is one of three companion directories, alongside [Blind Authors](https://jamalmazrui.github.io/BlindAuthors/) and [Blind Developers](https://jamalmazrui.github.io/BlindDevelopers/).

A presenter is included when they were blind or had low vision while creating the work, the work is contemporary (from the year 2010 onward), and it rises above a one-off clip to show sustained, notable leadership. In practice that means at least one of: a keynote or featured address at a recognized conference or event; an established, regularly published series; a body of at least three published works; or professional standing such as a broadcasting role, festival selection, or major recognition. Up to three works are shown per presenter, followed by a "Profiles:" line with a website, podcast, channel, LinkedIn, or Wikipedia article where one exists.

Presenters are listed alphabetically by last name, with the works grouped by type in an appendix.

## Presenters (Alphabetical by Last Name)

### Molly Burke {#pres-burke}
A content creator and public speaker, and the first blind creator to pass one million YouTube subscribers.
Profiles: [Website](https://www.mollyburkeofficial.com/), [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Molly_Burke)

#### [Molly Burke](https://www.mollyburkeofficial.com/) {#work-molly-burke}
A large body of online video and public speaking on lifestyle, advocacy, and life as a blind woman.

### Tommy Edison {#pres-edison}
A film critic and radio presenter known online as the Blind Film Critic.
Profiles: [Website](https://tommyedison.com/), [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tommy_Edison)

#### [The Blind Film Critic (2011)](https://tommyedison.com/) {#work-blind-film-critic}
A long-running YouTube series reviewing films by script, music, and sound, alongside candid talks about blindness.

### Lucy Edwards {#pres-edwards}
A broadcaster and content creator, and the first blind presenter on BBC Radio 1.
Profiles: [Website](https://www.lucyedwards.com/)

#### [Lucy Edwards](https://www.lucyedwards.com/) {#work-lucy-edwards}
Broadcasting and a large body of online video on lifestyle, beauty, and life without sight.

### Chad E. Foster {#pres-foster}
A corporate executive and international motivational speaker.
Profiles: [Website](https://chadefoster.com/)

#### [Keynote speaking](https://chadefoster.com/) {#work-foster-keynotes}
Keynote addresses on resilience and turning constraints into advantage, delivered to audiences worldwide.

### Haben Girma {#pres-girma}
An attorney, disability-rights advocate, and internationally sought keynote speaker.
Profiles: [Website](https://habengirma.com/), [LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/habengirma)

#### [Keynote addresses](https://habengirma.com/) {#work-girma-keynotes}
Keynote presentations for organizations worldwide, including a TIME100 Talk, on disability and innovation.

### Christine Ha {#pres-ha}
A chef, writer, and television host; the first blind winner of MasterChef.
Profiles: [Website](https://www.theblindcook.com/), [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christine_Ha)

#### [MasterChef (2012)](https://www.theblindcook.com/) {#work-masterchef}
Won the third season of MasterChef, then hosted and judged cooking television and gave TEDx and United Nations talks.

### Daniel Kish {#pres-kish}
An expert in human echolocation and founder of World Access for the Blind.
Profiles: [TED](https://www.ted.com/speakers/daniel_kish), [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Daniel_Kish)

#### [How I Use Sonar to Navigate the World (2015)](https://www.ted.com/speakers/daniel_kish) {#work-kish-ted}
A widely viewed TED talk on FlashSonar echolocation, part of a sustained body of talks and teaching.

### Jonathan Mosen {#pres-mosen}
A broadcaster and author of technology guides.
Profiles: [Website](https://mosen.org/)

#### [Access On](https://nfb.org/resources/publications-and-media/access-on-podcast) {#work-access-on}
Host of the National Federation of the Blind's weekly technology podcast.

### Shaun Preece {#pres-preece}
A broadcaster and co-host of the accessible-technology show Double Tap.
Profiles: [Website](https://doubletaponair.com/)

#### [Double Tap (2017)](https://doubletaponair.com/) {#work-double-tap-preece}
Co-hosts the daily accessible-technology podcast and radio show heard on AMI-audio.

### Steven Scott {#pres-scott}
A broadcaster who co-founded and hosts the accessible-technology show Double Tap.
Profiles: [Website](https://doubletaponair.com/)

#### [Double Tap (2017)](https://doubletaponair.com/) {#work-double-tap-scott}
Co-founded and hosts the daily accessible-technology podcast and radio show on AMI-audio.

### Sam Seavey {#pres-seavey}
A low-vision creator whose channel The Blind Life reviews assistive technology.
Profiles: [Podcast](https://theblindlife.podbean.com/)

#### [The Blind Life (2013)](https://theblindlife.podbean.com/) {#work-blind-life}
A long-running YouTube channel and podcast of assistive-technology reviews and low-vision tips.

### Jeff Thompson {#pres-thompson}
The founder and host of the Blind Abilities podcast.
Profiles: [Website](https://blindabilities.com/)

#### [Blind Abilities](https://blindabilities.com/) {#work-blind-abilities}
A podcast of interviews, demonstrations, and stories from a blindness perspective.

### Erik Weihenmayer {#pres-weihenmayer}
An adventurer and athlete, the first blind person to summit Mount Everest.
Profiles: [Website](https://erikweihenmayer.com/), [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Erik_Weihenmayer)

#### [Keynote addresses and the No Barriers film](https://erikweihenmayer.com/) {#work-weihenmayer}
Keynote speaking and the documentary chronicling his expeditions and the No Barriers movement.

### Peter White {#pres-white}
A BBC broadcaster and the corporation's disability affairs correspondent.
Profiles: [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peter_White_(broadcaster))

#### [In Touch](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/In_Touch_(radio_series)) {#work-in-touch}
Presents BBC Radio 4's long-running programme for blind and partially sighted listeners.
